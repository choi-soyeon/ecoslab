#### **<mark>For Potential Graduate Students (Master's, PhD)</mark>**

<blockquote style="font-size:1rem; font-style:normal;">
If you’re thinking about applying to join the ECoS lab, I’m most interested in working with students who are (1) excited about research ideas, (2) conscientious, and (3) comfortable learning as you go. Since the lab is new, there isn’t a single “right” fit. I’m open to different directions as long as your interests connect with <a href="{{ '/publications/' | relative_url }}">the kinds of questions I study</a>. Please <a href="mailto:choi.soyeon@yonsei.ac.kr">email me</a> a short note with your background and the research ideas you’re most excited to pursue. Here’s a <a href="https://psychsci.yonsei.ac.kr/%EB%8C%80%ED%95%99%EC%9B%90">link</a> to Yonsei’s graduate program.
</blockquote>

#### **<mark>For Potential Undergraduate Research Assistants</mark>**
<blockquote style="font-size:1rem; font-style:normal;">
I’ll be recruiting undergraduate RAs starting Spring 2026 for a variety of roles, including literature reviews, brainstorming, preparing study materials, data collection, and data analysis. Experience with social and/or evolutionary psychology and familiarity with R are a plus, but not required. Curiosity and enthusiasm for research matter most. If you are interested, please <a href="mailto:choi.soyeon@yonsei.ac.kr">email me</a> with a brief introduction of yourself and a few sentences about why you’d like to join the lab.
</blockquote>
